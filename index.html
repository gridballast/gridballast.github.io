Overview

GridBallast is a research project funded by ARPA-E and led by NRECA, the National Rural Electric Cooperative Association. It is developing low cost control devices for water heaters and residential circuits that can operate autonomously to improve the stability and resilience of the North American electric grid. Project partners include Carnegie Mellon University (CMU), Eaton, and SparkMeter.

Hardware

The GridBallast controllers measure grid frequency and voltage locally and control the loads to fix deviations from safe operating limits. An open source controller hardware platform shown below has been developed at CMU. A water heater controller has been developed at Eaton, and a residential circuit controller has been developed at SparkMeter.

Simulation

Before testing in the laboratory, we will run simulations to prove the safety and effectiveness of the controls. Simulations will be built on top of the Open Modeling Framework, an open source electric grid modeling platform (shown below in screenshots and a video walkthrough). Project Schedule

Development of the core algorithms and simulation results showing their effectiveness was completed in 2017. The hardware was developed in 2018. Field demonstrations at two rural electric cooperatives will be completed in 2019.
